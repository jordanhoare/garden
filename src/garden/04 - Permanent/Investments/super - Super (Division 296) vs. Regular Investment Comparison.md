### Assumptions

- Annual salary: $240,000
- Time period: 30 years (2025-2055)
- Investment return: 7% p.a. (before taxes) [based on historical S&P/ASX 200 returns](https://www.marketindex.com.au/asx200)
- Starting super balance: $0
- Inflation: Not factored (all figures in today's dollars)

## Key Insights

1. Even with Labor's changes, superannuation still provides significantly better wealth outcomes than regular investing.
2. The wealth reduction from Labor's policy for this example is approximately 4.2% over 30 years (disregarding super caps, indexation etc..)
3. The additional tax on balances above $3 million moderates but doesn't eliminate the substantial tax advantage of superannuation.
4. This represents "a shift from superannuation being a nearly unlimited tax-advantaged investment vehicle to one that still provides significant benefits but with some guardrails for very large balances."
5. The current draft legislation doesn't provide any mechanism "for individuals who are yet to retire to withdraw amounts from their superannuation" to reduce their balance below the $3 million threshold, further trapping those with illiquid assets..  but for the most part even with $30000 AUD invested yearly @ a 7% growth for 40 years - you're still unlikely to reach the $3 million figure.
