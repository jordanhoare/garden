### Labor's Approach

The Division 296 tax is calculated based on changes in an individual's Total Superannuation Balance (TSB), which inherently includes unrealised gains because it reflects current market value. [(source)](https://treasury.gov.au/sites/default/files/2023-09/c2023-443986-em.pdf)

One of the most controversial aspects of Labor's superannuation tax proposal is that it applies to both realised and unrealised gains for balances above $3 million and isn't indexed, as outlined in the [Treasury Laws Amendment (Better Targeted Superannuation Concessions) Bill 2023](https://treasury.gov.au/consultation/c2023-443986).

### Current Super Taxation

**Source**: [ATO superannuation taxation guidance](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/super-for-individuals-and-families/self-managed-super-funds-smsf/smsf-administration-and-reporting/how-smsfs-are-taxed#ato-Taxonincome)

Superannuation funds currently pay 15% tax on:

- Realised capital gains (when assets are sold)
- Income (dividends, interest, rent)

## Arguments Against Taxing Unrealised Gain

**Source**: [The Tax Institute submission on the Bill](https://www.taxinstitute.com.au/resources/submissions/2024/Treasury-Laws-Amendment-Better-Targeted-Superannuation-Concessions-and-Other-Measures-Bill-2023)

1. Individuals may need to pay tax on paper gains without having the cash flow to do so, creating **liquidity problems**
2. Determining **fair market value for illiquid assets** like property can be subjective
3. If **assets later fall in value**, tax may have been paid on gains that never materialised
4. Increased administrative obligations for superannuation funds to ensure members comply with the new measures

---

The policies structure - particularly the taxation of unrealised gains - seems like it will create a situation where people who accumulated large balances under previous more generous rules and invested in illiquid assets like property could face disproportionate impacts (almost to a point where the policy is likely to only target these individuals).
