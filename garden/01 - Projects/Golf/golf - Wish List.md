#### Tier 1 — Buy in the next 4 weeks

**1. Shot Scope V5 watch + tags** — Auto-tracks every shot, builds 12 months of data that drives all your future decisions.  https://www.golfbox.com.au/technology/shot-scope-v5-performance-tracker-black/

**2. Alignment sticks (×2)** — Get two — they're used in coaching drills constantly, and you'll need them at home and at the range.

**3. Permanent marker pens** — $5 AUD For marking balls, drawing lines on putting balls, identifying balls during rounds. Trivial cost, used constantly.

**Tier 1 total: ~$465-470**

#### Tier 2 — Buy in months 1-3

**4. Putting mat for home** — $150-300 AUD A genuinely good putting mat (Wellputt 3m, Big Moss, or PuttOUT Pro) lets you do daily 10-minute putting practice at home with no friction.

**5. Chipping mat for backyard** — $30-60 AUD A simple rubber chipping mat that protects your lawn (and shoes) when chipping at home.

**6. Chipping/practice net** — $80-150 AUD Combines with the chipping mat for backyard short-game work. Brands: Spornia SPG-7, Rukket Haack, GoSports. Dimensions matter — you want at least a 7×7 net for reasonable chipping practice.

**7. Practice balls for home** — $30 AUD Almost Golf practice balls or Birdie Balls. They fly real distances at 1/3 speed, won't break windows or annoy neighbors. 12-pack is enough for most backyard sessions.

**Tier 2 total: ~$290-540**

#### Tier 3 — Buy in months 3-6 if you continue committing

**8. Microfiber cleaning towel + brush** — $25-40 AUD For grooves and clubface cleaning. You probably bought one with initial accessories, but if not, this is essential for wedge spin consistency.

**9. Decent rangefinder OR upgrade to Shot Scope GPS** — $300-500 AUD Once you're playing more rounds and starting to think about course management decisions, knowing exact yardages matters. Bushnell Tour V5 Shift or Garmin Approach Z82 are reliable options.

Honest reality check: **Wembley has GPS on carts, and Shot Scope V5 already gives you basic distances.** A standalone rangefinder is a "nice to have" not "need to have" until you're playing more demanding courses or competitions. Defer.

**10. Golf-specific resistance bands or training aids** — $40-80 AUD Once you're working specifically on swing changes, training aids like alignment rods, impact bags, or resistance bands can help reinforce mechanics outside the facility. Examples: SKLZ Gold Flex, alignment rod harness, Tour Striker. Wait until your coach recommends one specifically.
