
## Australian Greens

- The Greens propose implementing a **wealth tax** on individuals with net wealth exceeding $1 billion and introducing a **40% tax on corporations** with over $100 million in turnover. This policy is designed to address rising inequality and generate substantial revenue for public services such as healthcare and education. The Greens estimate this will raise an additional **$5 billion annually** and help fund social programs. ([source](https://www.news.com.au/national/politics/greens-push-to-hit-australian-rich-listers-with-billionaires-tax/news-story/029ccd24524bf48d7988bd51b87930ae))
- For **public health**, the Greens propose expanding **Medicare** to include **dental, optical, and mental health** services, which would require an additional **$15 billion annually**. This would represent about **30% of the current annual Medicare budget** and make healthcare services more universally accessible. ([source](https://greens.org.au/platform))
- On **housing affordability**, the Greens plan to invest **$10 billion** to build **100,000 new homes** over the next 10 years for first-time buyers. This initiative would constitute roughly **1.5% of the annual federal budget**. They also advocate for **rent freezes** and **housing price controls** to ensure affordability for renters. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/may/03/who-should-can-i-vote-for-australia-federal-election-policy-guide-labor-liberal-coalition-greens-policies))
- The Greens propose a **national, fully funded public transport network**, with a focus on **electric trains** and **hydrogen buses**. The funding needed to support these projects is estimated at **$8 billion annually** over 10 years, making up **1% of the current federal budget**. ([source](https://greens.org.au/platform))
- **Environmental reform** is a central focus, with the Greens seeking to allocate **1% of the federal budget ($5 billion annually)** into a **Green New Deal** to fund renewable energy infrastructure, energy efficiency programs, and **climate adaptation** efforts. Their goal is a **100% renewable energy economy by 2030**. ([source](https://www.news.com.au/national/politics/greens-push-to-hit-australian-rich-listers-with-billionaires-tax/news-story/029ccd24524bf48d7988bd51b87930ae))
- The Greens propose a **carbon tax on fossil fuel companies**, aiming to raise **$10 billion per year**. The policy would ensure that polluting industries contribute to the transition to renewable energy and environmental protection efforts. ([source)](https://greens.org.au/explainers/cprs)
- They also support the **abolition of fossil fuel subsidies**, reallocating this funding towards renewable energy development. This policy is expected to free up **$4 billion per year**, which would then be reinvested into green infrastructure. ([source](https://greens.org.au/platform))
- The Greens advocate for creating a **sovereign wealth fund**, similar to Norway's **Government Pension Fund Global (GPFG)**, through **resource revenues**, aimed at providing financial stability for future generations. This would require an additional **$5 billion annually**. ([source](https://www.pbo.gov.au/elections))

## Australian Labor Party (ALP)

- **Taxation reforms** under the ALP include a **modest reduction in income taxes** for middle-income earners, costing approximately **$20 billion over 10 years**. This would increase the **annual tax expenditure** by **7.5%**. They plan to fund these cuts through an increase in **corporate taxes**, particularly targeting **large corporations** with annual revenues over $50 million. ([source](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-03-28/election-2025-key-promises-labor-coalition/104717394))
- The ALP proposes a **$8.5 billion increase** in **Medicare** spending over 10 years, aimed at expanding **bulk billing**, **prescription subsidies**, and reducing out-of-pocket costs for health services. This would increase **Medicare’s budget by 16%**, bringing it up to about **$62 billion annually**. ([source](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-03-28/election-2025-key-promises-labor-coalition/104717394))
- The ALP has pledged **$10 billion** over the next decade to build **100,000 affordable homes** as part of their **Help to Buy** scheme, which will provide **shared equity** for first-time buyers. This investment would represent **1.5% of the total federal budget**. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/may/03/who-should-can-i-vote-for-australia-federal-election-policy-guide-labor-liberal-coalition-greens-policies))
- In terms of **renewable energy**, Labor plans to invest **$2.3 billion** into **household battery subsidies** and **energy efficiency programs**. This would represent a **5% increase** in their energy budget, helping to achieve a goal of **82% renewable energy by 2030**. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/may/03/who-should-can-i-vote-for-australia-federal-election-policy-guide-labor-liberal-coalition-greens-policies))
- Labor has committed to expanding **public transport infrastructure** with **$6 billion** over 10 years to support major projects in Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane, helping to reduce congestion and pollution. This would represent approximately **0.75% of the annual government budget**. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/may/03/who-should-can-i-vote-for-australia-federal-election-policy-guide-labor-liberal-coalition-greens-policies))
- The ALP’s fiscal policies include increasing **government spending** on **infrastructure** and **social programs** while introducing **higher corporate taxes** on larger companies. The total increase in government spending is projected to rise from **26.2% to 27% of GDP** over the next decade, driven largely by social services and infrastructure. ([source](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-03-25/federal-budget-2025-chalmers-tax-cuts-election-clash/105093346))

## Liberal-National Coalition

- The Coalition proposes **$15 billion in tax cuts** for middle-income earners, which will cost around **4% of total annual government expenditure**. This is designed to stimulate consumption by providing more disposable income to Australians. These cuts are funded by **reductions in public sector jobs** and **climate program cuts**. ([source](https://www.news.com.au/national/federal-election/peter-duttons-final-plea-the-question-you-need-to-answer-today/news-story/7cfca97914da423eb444934145806bcc))
- A core element of the Coalition’s **energy policy** involves investing **$36.4 billion in nuclear energy** development over the next 10 years, equating to **12% of the annual government budget**. This policy is part of their broader plan to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and transition to cleaner sources of energy. The funding will be sourced from **defense and energy sector budgets**. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/live/2025/may/01/australia-election-2025-live-anthony-albanese-peter-dutton-campaign-policies-economy-inflation-cost-of-living-coalition-labor-ntwnfb))
- In **mental health**, the Coalition plans to invest an additional **$500 million** into **free mental health consultations** for all Australians, expanding existing mental health programs. This would increase funding by **30%** in this area, with the money to be sourced from **defense and foreign aid budget cuts**. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/may/03/who-should-can-i-vote-for-australia-federal-election-policy-guide-labor-liberal-coalition-greens-policies))
- On **climate change**, the Coalition proposes a **$10 billion cut in climate programs**, redirecting those resources toward **national defense** and economic stimulus measures, including **small business tax cuts**. This reduction would represent **4% of the climate program budget** and reallocate funding to **national security**. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/live/2025/may/01/australia-election-2025-live-anthony-albanese-peter-dutton-campaign-policies-economy-inflation-cost-of-living-coalition-labor-ntwnfb))
- The **fiscal plan** includes a **$14 billion improvement** in the **federal budget deficit** over four years and a **$40 billion reduction** in gross national debt by 2030. These savings will come from **public sector wage cuts**, **foreign aid reductions**, and other budget cuts aimed at reducing government spending. ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/live/2025/may/01/australia-election-2025-live-anthony-albanese-peter-dutton-campaign-policies-economy-inflation-cost-of-living-coalition-labor-ntwnfb))

## Observations

- The **Greens** advocate for a larger, progressive taxation system, focusing on wealth redistribution and funding social programs like universal healthcare and housing, with significant investment in renewable energy. Their fiscal policy includes a reallocation of funds from fossil fuel subsidies towards green infrastructure.
- **Labor** prioritizes tax cuts for the middle class while increasing spending on healthcare and renewable energy programs. Their plan includes a notable increase in public spending, financed through higher taxes on corporations and the wealthy.
- The **Coalition** focuses on middle-class tax relief, investing in nuclear energy, and expanding mental health services. They are aiming for fiscal conservatism by cutting public sector jobs, defense spending, and reducing climate-related funding.
